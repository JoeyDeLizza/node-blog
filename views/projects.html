Raycaster

A raycaster is a computer graphics technique used to render three-dimensional scenes in a two-dimensional space. It is particularly known for its application in early first-person shooter games, where it revolutionized the way virtual environments were displayed.

Doom is one of my favorite games and I had plans to create a game with this project, but I've turned towards learning about modern rendering systems instead. It was a cool thing to implement but I'd rather be working on projects where I can learn things that are relevant

Because of the nature of Moores law, anything that an extremley clever graphics programmer can do at one point can be replicated by a merely competent programmer some years later.

John Carmack
